bkvagnini Portfolio Site Case Study

Who:

UX Designers / Hiring Managers

What:

Portfolio Review

When:

A few weeks

Where:

Desktop and Mobile Phone Browser

How:

Paper and Pencil, Figma, Visual Studio Code

Steps

User Research

I watched numerous videos on YouTube from Senior UX Designers who oftentimes have to hire additional UX Designers.

Notable quotes include:

"You want to know that they can solve a product challenge."
"I want to be able to see their thinking process" - "the last thing I want to see is just some pretty mockups of something, cuz that won't tell me whether this person was able to solve a problem or not"

Insights gained

The ability to solve a problem with compelling text, as well as visuals, is the most important thing. The visual part is easy; the thinking behind the visuals (the thought process) is harder to get across.

Ideation

I wanted to be able to use this portfolio site as a multi-portfolio site; hence the sub-categories page.
In addition, it showcases that I'm more of a utility player, vs. a specialist.
My skill set is pretty diverse. This portfolio site helps to showcase some of that.

Prototyping

Created the mockup in Figma

Retrospective

There are some differences between the final product and the Figma mockups. Since I was the sole person making decisions, it seemed faster to incorporate them directly into the final product, vs. taking the time to modify the mockup.
Once the site starts getting reviewed, I would incorporate those suggestions into the next version iteration.
